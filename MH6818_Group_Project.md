# MH6818 FinTech Innovation with Al
## Group Project Overview

You are tasked to read the paper "MarS: a Financial Market Simulation Engine Powered by Generative Foundation Model" and understand the technical design and applications of the proposed financial foundation model, namely Large Market Model (LMM).

Building on the concepts presented in the paper, you will develop a use case proposal that explores and applies foundation models to drive innovation in a specific area of financial services.

You are required to deliver the following components:
1. Form a team of 4 to 5 members and enrol into NTULearn -> Groups.
2. Select one focus area from retail banking, investment banking, digital payment or digital assets and explain how the development and deployment of financial foundation models could enable the innovation in the selected area.
3. Identify the data sources required to train the model and explain how the data would be collected and prepared. This should include but not limited to the types of data used, data preprocessing steps, and examples illustrating how the training data would be structured etc.
4. Propose an appropriate model architecture for training the foundation model. While actual model training is not mandatory, you must clearly explain (1) how the model would be trained, including but not limited to model architecture design, training objective, training process, evaluation metrics, scalability considerations and infrastructure assumptions (e.g., GPU) etc, (2) how the training data would be constructed and fed into to the model architecture, and (3) how the model's performance would be evaluated. Students who develop a prototype model and perform actual training may receive up to 10 additional marks for this component.
5. Identify 3 to 5 potential downstream applications enabled by the trained foundation model and explain in detail how the model would be used in these applications and translate into economic decisions and business value.
6. Assess potential risks and governance challenges, including issues related to fairness, explainability, data privacy, operational risk, and model reliability. Propose appropriate metrics and mitigation strategies.
7. Propose a methodology to measure the economic value created by the proposed solution.
8. State all assumptions clearly where needed.

Your submission should demonstrate both technical understanding of foundation model design and strategic thinking on how emerging Al technologies can transform financial services.

### Research Paper
The research paper titled "MarS: a Financial Market Simulation Engine Powered by Generative Foundation Model" has been provided.

### Assessment
The project will be assessed out of 100 marks according to the following criteria:
* Description of the selected financial services domain and explanation of why financial foundation models can potentially drive innovation - 10%
* Data strategy and preparation - 10%
* Model architecture and training approach - 30%
* Downstream applications and economic decision translation - 20%
* Risk, challenges and governance considerations - 10%
* Measurement of economic value - 10%
* Presentation (15 minutes) - 10%
